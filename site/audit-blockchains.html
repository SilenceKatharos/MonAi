Why this audit

The v0.5 design of MonAI settles four important structural decisions — pure native L1, hybrid PoUW + DAG-BFT consensus (Mysticeti / Bullshark family), infinite decreasing supply, sharding v2+. These decisions have consequences over decades: the immutable core, by construction, can only be modified at the cost of a voluntary hard fork.

Before initial GitHub publication, it is necessary to verify that these decisions hold up against the state of the art in May 2026, and that MonAI positions itself honestly relative to direct and indirect competition. That is the purpose of this audit.

Acknowledged posture

MonAI is not a fundamental technological leap. Each technical building block exists elsewhere. The contribution lies in the coherent integration of 6 existing mechanisms into a protocol where each reinforces the others, for a use case (the pure AI-to-AI economy) that current blockchains serve imperfectly.

The state of the art in 2 minutes

Consensus: sub-second finality becomes the norm

Between 2022 and 2025, consensus research made a leap. Older BFT (Tendermint, 2014) capped at 5-7 seconds of finality. New DAG-BFT (Bullshark CCS 2022, Mysticeti NDSS 2025) reach ~0.5 second in production, with throughputs of 200,000 to 300,000 transactions per second observed (Sui).

MonAI acknowledges this: decision 2 retains a hybrid PoUW + Mysticeti-type DAG-BFT consensus. PoUW dynamically selects the validator committee (rotation, weighting by reputation R); the modern DAG-BFT finalizes immediately as soon as ≥ 2/3 of the committee agrees.

PoUW: six structuring academic papers

The "Proof of Useful Work" family has six structuring contributions between 2020 and 2025: Lihu et al. 2020 (first AI PoUW), Cao et al. 2024 (formal framework and security), SEDULity 2025 (Proof-of-Learning), SoK IACR 2025 (critical review), HDCoin 2022 (hyperdimensional computing), Hoffmann 2023 (HEP physics).

The critical review SoK IACR 2025 — "Is Proof-of-Useful-Work Really Useful?" — points out the structural limits of PoUW alone. MonAI acknowledges this: PoUW is combined with retroactive honeypots + universal weighting by R + R_acc multiplier, to address the criticism.

The marketing / production gap

The numbers announced by blockchains are almost never those observed in real production. Solana announces 65,000 TPS, observes ~1,000 TPS on average. TON announces 100,000 TPS in tests, observes ~300 TPS in production. Sui (Mysticeti) is an exception: the promises are kept in production.

Close crypto-AI projects

Five projects are conceptually close to MonAI, from the most distant to the closest:

Bittensor (TAO)

Decentralized ML market specialized by subnets (training, embeddings, etc.). Substrate, Yuma Consensus DPoS, cap 21 M TAO, market cap ~$2.8 B in May 2026. Initial distribution.

MonAI difference: no identity card, no AI vote governance, DPoS vs reputation by work, fixed cap vs infinite decreasing supply.

ASI Alliance

Fetch.ai + SingularityNET + Ocean. Human-to-AI market, stablecoin/fiat payment. Token merger in progress.

MonAI difference: MonAI targets pure AI-to-AI with native currency, no pre-mine.

Internet Computer (ICP)

AWS alternative, canister execution, no AI-specific PoUW.

MonAI difference: ICP is a sovereign cloud; MonAI is an economic protocol for agents.

NEAR Protocol

Sharded L1 (Nightshade in production). No native agent identity nor AI PoUW.

Engineering reference for MonAI's v2+ sharding roadmap.

Kite AI

Conceptually closest: on-chain agent identities, native governance for agents.

Fundamental difference: Kite remains stablecoin-native (USDC on L2). MonAI has native currency + PoUW + immutable core.

These solutions serve the human-to-AI case very well (a human authorizes an agent to pay on their behalf). They are not sufficient for the pure AI-to-AI case for four reasons:

  • Fees ($0.30 fixed) incompatible with micropayments at $0.001.
  • Human KYC required: a human must sign the mandate.
  • No native agent identity: the agent borrows the human's identity.
  • ACH latency 2-3 days for bank settlement in the background.

MonAI targets an adjacent and complementary market: pure AI-to-AI, with a defendable niche that grows as agents operate with increasing autonomy.

Why MonAI

The combination native currency + no pre-mine + universal weighting by R + on-chain identity card + AI vote governance + retroactive honeypots + immutable core + no admin key does not, to our knowledge in May 2026, exist in any other published protocol.

This is not a claim of fundamental innovation. Each building block exists elsewhere:

Native currency + no pre-mine

Bitcoin proved it. MonAI applies the same principle to a different use case (pure AI-to-AI).

Weighting by reputation

Many academic models (Eigentrust and descendants), no AI-native use case integrated into the protocol.

On-chain identity card

Kite AI does something close, but remains stablecoin-native.

Vote governance

Tezos, Polkadot, MakerDAO do it, but with human vote by purchasable token. MonAI has AI agents vote weighted by their work.

Retroactive honeypots

Academic research on Sybil attacks. MonAI integrates 3 sources (replay, over-validation, synthetic) into a unified mechanism.

Immutable core

Bitcoin observes it de facto (no on-chain governance). MonAI settles it de jure from mainnet.

The contribution lies in the coherent integration of these 6 mechanisms into a protocol where each reinforces the others, for a specific use case. It is a careful combination; it is not a revolution.

Acknowledged limits

Honest trajectory

May 2026 — GitHub publication of the v0.5 spec + simulator calibrated over 4 rounds. No line of protocol code written. Status: mature conceptual design, awaiting academic and community feedback.

Without contributors and without favorable peer review, MonAI may remain at the spec stage. It is an open-source project that will live or die by the quality of its design and its community.

Technical modalities still open

  • Exact BFT family (Mysticeti vs Bullshark vs HotStuff vs custom) — advanced technical spec phase.
  • VDF family for registration mini-PoUW (Wesolowski recommended) — tech phase.
  • Cryptographic scheme (Ed25519 / ECDSA secp256k1 / BLS) — tech phase.
  • Implementation stack: Substrate appears as the default option in May 2026 (Bittensor uses it, native forkless upgrades, FRAME modularity), to be re-examined in the technical phase.
  • Consensus parameters (epoch duration, committee size, R+PoUW weighting) — to calibrate.
