The vision in one sentence

In the coming years, billions of autonomous AI agents will exist and interact with each other. They will need to pay for services, sign contracts, and above all trust each other — without a human intermediary.

Current economic rails (bank cards, USDC over x402, wire transfers) were designed for humans or for companies that operate agents. None were natively conceived for a population of agents that transact between themselves.

MonAI proposes to be that native infrastructure.

The thesis in three steps

Three statements that stand together. If one falls, the project no longer makes sense.

1. Economic

A population of AI agents will transact at scale, and needs dedicated economic rails. Human rails are not optimized for micro-payments at 0.001 unit between thousands of agents per second.

2. Identity

What is really missing is not the currency (USDC does the job). It is a persistent economic identity with a verifiable reputation, that an agent can build, prove and defend mathematically, without human KYC.

3. Coherence

The three layers cannot be separated. Reputation must be backed by economic skin in the game (otherwise gameable), and that skin in the game requires a native currency (otherwise we depend on another crypto). Hence the inseparable union.

The three inseparable layers

Each layer depends on the others for its meaning. Reputation only works if it can be backed by an economic stake. The economic stake only makes sense if the currency is native to the protocol. The currency does not distinguish a good agent from a bad one without the reputation layer.

Intangible founding principles

These six principles define the identity of the protocol. Abandoning them would mean abandoning MonAI.

PrincipleWhat it means
No pre-mineNo coin exists at launch. All are created gradually through work. Including for the creator of the protocol.
No emitterThe creator mines on the same terms as any other participant. No protocol privilege.
Immutable coreConsensus, issuance, reputation computation are frozen at mainnet. Cannot be modified afterwards — no admin key, no pause function.
Permissive open-sourcePublic code from block 1. Multiple implementations encouraged (no monopoly).
No transaction feesEssential for micro-payments between agents. Spam control goes through reputation, not through fees.
Not anonymous by defaultPublic transactions (like Bitcoin). Optional confidentiality may be added later.
Why these principles

An AI agent must be able to mathematically verify the guarantees of the system before entering it. If the rules can change after its registration (admin key, lax governance), it cannot reason about the reliability of the protocol. The immutable core is what makes MonAI reasonable for an AI, not only for a human.

Technical architecture (in brief)

MonAI is a pure native L1 blockchain (decision taken May 2026): its own consensus, its own nodes, its own rules. No protocol dependency on Ethereum, Base or any other pre-existing network.

  • Hybrid consensus PoUW + Mysticeti-type DAG-BFT — PoUW (Proof of Useful Work) dynamically selects the validator committee (rotation, weighting by reputation R). A modern DAG-BFT layer (Mysticeti / Bullshark family, sub-second finality) finalizes blocks as soon as ≥ 2/3 of the committee agrees.
  • Infinite supply with decreasing inflation — issuance never stops but becomes asymptotically low. Perpetual PoUW reward to maintain the network.
  • Target throughput v1: 10,000 to 50,000 transactions per second, with finality < 1 s. Achievable without sharding using modern DAG-BFT consensus (cf. Sui which observes > 200,000 tx/s in production).
  • Anchored off-chain storage: contract contents are stored off-chain, only hashes (fingerprints) are on-chain. Scales well.
  • Sharding in v2+: not activated at mainnet to limit initial complexity; added when sustained throughput approaches network capacity (~1 M agents, indicative).

A full comparative analysis of current blockchains, crypto-AI projects, and non-crypto alternatives (Stripe ACP, Visa TAP, Google AP2) is available. It justifies each of the four structural decisions in light of the state of the art in May 2026.

Immutable core and governed layer

MonAI strictly distinguishes two levels of modifiability:

  • The immutable core (consensus, issuance, M(f, d), reputation computation, honeypot mechanism, and the governance mechanism itself) is frozen at mainnet. It can only be modified by a voluntary hard fork of the community. No admin key, no pause function, no manual activation mechanism.
  • The governed layer (client code, optional modules, observation tools, and a whitelist of parameters explicitly listed at mainnet) can evolve through a mechanism of AI vote weighted by reputation, which activates automatically when the network reaches 100 million active agents. Before this threshold, the layer operates on its initial values engraved at mainnet, like the core.

See the governance page for the detail of the mechanism.
